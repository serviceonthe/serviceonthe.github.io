About The Organiser

TThe organiser of the Bangladesh Capital Market Fair in London is CURRY LIFE EVENTS, which is part of the Curry Life Magazine Group.

The Curry Life Magazine is a long-established voice of Bangladeshi restaurateurs in Britain and in the continental Europe. It reaches to some 12,000 Indian restaurants in the UK alone, with an estimated readership of over 100,000. The restaurants and the catering trade is the main source of income for vast majority of Non Resident Bangladeshis living in the UK and Europe. Over the years the Curry Life has established deep-rooted trust and connection with the NRBs, particularly those working in the Hospitality and Catering Sector.

For the last 10 years Curry Life has been successfully organising Trade Fairs, Food Festivals, Business Networking Events, Exhibitions, Competitions and Awards Ceremonies in different countries, Including the UK, Spain, Italy, Germany, Sweden, Norway, USA, Middle East, Malaysia, India and Bangladesh.

About Me

JSyed Belal Ahmed

Syed Belal Ahmed is Bangladeshi-born British entrepreneur. He has over 25 years of experience working in the government and private sectors in areas such as marketing, media, event management, printing and publishing.

He manages 11 business portfolios, including restaurants, property development, and event management, tourism, and media publication, in the UK and in Bangladesh.

Mr. Ahmed co-founded the Nandan Group of Companies in Bangladesh, which includes Nandan Park and Nandan Megashop supermarkets in Dhaka. He serves as the Managing Director.

He serves as the Editor of Curry Life Magazine and he is the founder Chairman of the UK-Bangladesh Trade Council (UKBBC).

Syed Nahas Pasha

Syed Nahas Pasha, one of the most respected British-Bangladeshi journalists in the UK, is the Chief Editor of the Weekly Janomot, a hugely successful Bangla newspaper. He is also Chief Editor of Curry Life Magazine.

Mr. Pasha has long experience in event management, networking conference, exhibitions, print and publishing. He is one of the founding members, and former General Secretary of London Bangla Press Club which acts as a voice for British Bangladeshi journalists. He is a member of the National Union of Journalists (NUJ) and sits on the executive committee of the Commonwealth Journalists Association (CJA-UK).

Mr Pasha has worked on numerous policies and issues with government bodies including the Home Office and Foreign and Commonwealth Office. He has been invited to travel with various British Government delegations to Europe and Bangladesh.
